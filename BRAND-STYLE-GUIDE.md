## 1. Brand Identity

### Who Shana Is
Shana Gates is a Coachella Valley REALTOR® at **Craft & Bauer | Real Broker**. She is a trusted local expert — warm, knowledgeable, and genuinely invested in the desert community. She is not a hard-sell agent. She is the neighbor who knows everything about every neighborhood and helps people make smart decisions.

### Brand Personality
- **Sophisticated** but never cold or corporate
- **Local authority** — deeply knowledgeable about Coachella Valley history, lifestyle, and market
- **Warm and approachable** — feels like advice from a trusted friend, not a sales pitch
- **Cinematic and premium** — visuals skew toward editorial, not stock-photo generic

### Brand Tagline (use sparingly)
> *Your Coachella Valley Expert*

## 3. Typography

### Font Stack

| Role | Font | Fallback | Weight |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Display / Headlines** | Cormorant Garamond | Georgia, serif | 300–400 |
| **Body / UI** | Jost | system-ui, sans-serif | 400–500 |
| **Supporting / Brand** | Montserrat | system-ui, sans-serif | 100–900 |

### Thumbnail & Social Graphic Typography (what you see in video thumbnails)

| Element | Style | Weight |
|---|---|---|
| **Eyebrow label** (e.g. "INSIDE") | Montserrat, all caps, wide tracking (0.3em+) | 500 |
| **Main headline word** (e.g. "FRANK SINATRA'S") | Montserrat Condensed Bold or Impact-style, all caps | 800–900 |
| **Script accent** (e.g. "Palm Springs") | Cursive/script overlay, turquoise bright `#4ECDC4` | — |
| **Subtitle badge** | Montserrat, all caps, tight tracking | 600–700 |
| **"Shana Gates" name** | Script font, white or turquoise | — |
| **"REAL ESTATE" sub-label** | Montserrat, all caps, wide tracking | 500 |

### Letter-Spacing Reference

| Context | Value |
|---|---|
| Hero eyebrow / section labels | `0.32–0.35em` |
| Navigation links | `0.20em` |
| Button text | `0.20–0.22em` |
| Fact labels / captions | `0.04–0.05em` |
| Body copy | `0 or -0.01em` |

### Typography Rules
- Headlines on dark backgrounds: **Cream `#F2EDE4` or White `#FFFFFF`**
- Never bold Cormorant Garamond — it's a light/thin serif by design; its elegance lives at weight 300–400
- Montserrat at weight 800+ condensed is used for impact-headline moments only (thumbnails, hero graphics)
- Mixed-font headlines: serif for the elegant word, sans for the punchy word

## 4. Logo Usage

### Shana Gates Wordmark
- "Shana" in a flowing script — white or turquoise on dark backgrounds
- "GATES" in Montserrat all-caps beneath it, wide tracking
- "REAL ESTATE" in Montserrat all-caps, smaller, wide tracking
- Palm tree icon (circle) used as a standalone mark

### Craft & Bauer | Real Broker
- Always include both "Craft & Bauer" and "| Real Broker" when using the brokerage name
- The C&B logo uses a serif typeface (similar to Cormorant) in C&B Teal `#1A4447`
- The "real" wordmark (boxed, mirrored) always appears beneath the C&B name
- On dark backgrounds: use the white/cream version of the logo
- On light backgrounds: use the teal `#1A4447` version
- Minimum clear space: equal to the height of the "C" in "Craft" on all sides

### Logo Don'ts
- Never stretch, rotate, or recolor the logos
- Never place the C&B logo on a busy photo without a background buffer
- Never use the bronze color as a substitute for the C&B teal

## 5. Photography & Imagery Style

### Hero Photography
- **Desert landscapes**: golden hour light, mountain backdrops, palm trees silhouetted
- **Architecture**: mid-century modern, clean lines, pool shots, wide angles
- **Lifestyle**: warm, aspirational — outdoor living, al fresco dining, golf, resort pools
- **Shana photos**: professional headshots with natural light, outdoor or desert backdrop

### Thumbnail / Graphic Imagery Style
- **Dark cinematic base**: photos treated with dark overlay (0.4–0.7 opacity dark vignette)
- **Layered compositions**: background photo + foreground cutout of Shana + text overlay
- **Historical thumbnails**: archival B&W photos work well layered against modern desert architecture shots
- **Color grading**: slight cool teal shift on background, warm on Shana cutout
- **Always include Shana** in corner or side of thumbnail for brand recognition

### Image Don'ts
- No generic stock photos of non-desert architecture or non-California homes
- No overly bright, washed-out, or flat-lit photos
- No clip art, no cartoony illustrations
- No photos that include competing brokerage signage

## 6. Voice & Tone

### Core Principles
1. **Expert neighbor, not salesperson** — Write as the most knowledgeable person in the room who genuinely wants to help, never push
2. **Specific and local** — Always anchor to Coachella Valley, specific cities, neighborhoods, addresses when possible
3. **Direct** — Lead with the answer. Don't bury the point in a preamble
4. **Warm but not bubbly** — No exclamation points unless truly warranted. No "Amazing!" or "Super excited to share!"

### Sentence-Level Rules
- Open blog posts with the most interesting or surprising fact — not "Did you know..."
- Use "you/your" to speak directly to the reader
- Avoid: *leverage, synergy, seamless, holistic, game-changing, cutting-edge, thrilled, excited to announce*
- Use: *smart, straightforward, local, specific numbers, named streets and neighborhoods*
- End social captions with a question or genuine invitation, not a hard CTA

### Platform Voice Adjustments

| Platform | Tone | Length | Hashtags |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Instagram** | Warm, visual-led, storytelling | 3–5 sentences | 5–8 relevant tags |
| **Facebook** | Conversational, community-focused | 2–4 sentences + link | Minimal (0–3) |
| **TikTok** | Energetic, hook-forward, informal | Hook line + 2–3 sentences | 8–12 trending + local |
| **LinkedIn** | Professional, data-led, investor focus | 4–6 sentences | 3–5 professional tags |
| **Threads** | Casual, opinion or question | 1–2 sentences | None or 1–2 |
| **YouTube** | Authoritative, educational, full narrative | Full description + chapters | N/A |
| **Blog** | Journalist/local-expert voice | 500–900 words | SEO keywords only |

## 8. Graphic Design System

### Card / Badge Design
- Rounded corners: `12px` for cards, `100px` for pill badges, `8px` for buttons
- Dark surface backgrounds: `#141414` or `#1A1A1A`
- Hover/active state: bronze border `#B8975A` at 1.5px
- Selected/active state: `rgba(184,151,90,0.06)` background fill

### Button Design
- Primary: Bronze `#B8975A` background, black `#000` text, `8px` radius, Montserrat 600, `0.22em` letter-spacing
- Secondary: Transparent background, bronze border, bronze text
- Hover: 85% opacity on primary; bronze border on secondary

### Dividers
- Use `rgba(255,255,255,0.08)` for subtle lines on dark backgrounds
- Use the bronze at low opacity `rgba(184,151,90,0.18)` for accent dividers
- Never use harsh white or gray lines

### Shadow System
- Cards: `box-shadow: 0 4px 24px rgba(26,24,20,0.08)`
- Hover elevation: `box-shadow: 0 8px 32px rgba(0,0,0,0.55)`
- Bronze glow (active/featured): `box-shadow: 0 0 20px rgba(184,151,90,0.55)`

## 9. Video Style

### HeyGen Avatar
- Shana appears in professional attire against a neutral/branded background
- Voice is warm, confident, conversational — not broadcast-newscaster
- Videos are 60–90 seconds for Reels/TikTok, 3–5 minutes for YouTube

### Video Caption / Lower-Third Formula
- White headline text on semi-transparent dark overlay
- Bronze accent for emphasis words
- Montserrat for all on-screen text

### B-roll Style
- Aerial desert footage, palm tree lines, mountain panoramas
- Mid-century modern architecture details
- Pool and outdoor living shots
- Never: generic city skylines, non-CV footage, cheesy real estate drone clichés
